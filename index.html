The "Life Sport"

My old boss boss told me that tennis is a sport passed down through mothers. This is partially true in my case. I grew up hearing stories about my Grandmother bringing my dad in a stroller to the public tennis courts in the Bronx when he was an infant. This early exposure must have planted the seed of his obsession for this sport, which he later passed down to me. Tennis is often referred to as a "life sport" because people often play it well into their twilight years. That was true for my grandmother, who played into her eighties and for my dad, who has been playing essentially non-stop from ages three into his sixties. I think of tennis as a "life sport" in the sense that people tend to revolve their lives around the game once they fall in love with it.
