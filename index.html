The "Life Sport"

My old boss told me that tennis is a sport passed down through mothers. This is partially true in my case. I grew up hearing stories about my grandmother bringing my dad in a stroller to the public tennis courts in the Bronx when he was just an infant. That early exposure must have planted the seed of his obsession for this sport, which he later passed down to me.

Tennis is often referred to as a "life sport" because people play it well into their twilight years. That was certainly true for my grandmother, who played into her eighties, and for my dad, who has been playing essentially non-stop from age three into his sixties. Once you fall in love with this game, it tends to revolve around your life — not the other way around.

My Tennis Story

Growing up watching my dad play, tennis always felt like part of the family fabric. He brought me out to hit from a young age, and I soon became intrigued by the sport. Playing tennis felt like it was part of my family heritage and in my DNA.

When I got to high school I joined the team and had lackluster results. I played for one season, had a decent time, but at that point I was more interested in hanging out with friends on the far more social cross country team. Tennis went on the back burner, and for a stretch of years it stayed there.

The rediscovery came when I started managing The Palisades Tennis Center. Suddenly I was surrounded by tennis again. Pristine courts, players, coaches, the whole ecosystem of it. I call this the beginning of my "Tennis Renaissance."Being immersed in that environment every day reignited my dormant passion. I started hitting again, first casually, then obsessively. I remembered exactly what I had loved about it as a kid watching my dad: the variety, the competition, the way a single good shot can make you forget every bad one before it.

Since then, tennis has become one of the central threads of my life. This site is a record of some of my tennis adventures; the courts I’ve played on, the tournaments I’ve been lucky enough to attend, and the players who have inspired me along the way.
